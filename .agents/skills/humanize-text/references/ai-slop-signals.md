# AI-Slop Signals

Use this file as a diagnostic checklist, not as an authorship detector.
A passage can show several of these signals and still be human-written. Some concise, non-native, or highly standardized writing will also trigger them.

## Strong signals to inspect

### 1. Generic scaffolding before substance
The text spends words setting up the point instead of making it.

Examples:
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- "This highlights the significance of..."

### 2. Repetitive signposting
The passage leans on transitions rather than logical flow.

Watch for stacks like:
- furthermore
- moreover
- additionally
- in conclusion
- overall
- on the other hand

### 3. Vague abstraction
The text uses abstract nouns where the reader needs concrete actors, actions, or outcomes.

Watch for clusters like:
- innovation
- optimization
- enhancement
- solution
- process
- efficiency
- value
- impact

### 4. Low information density
Many sentences are grammatical but expendable. Removing them does not change meaning.

Test:
- Cut the sentence.
- If the paragraph still says the same thing, the sentence was probably padding.

### 5. Symmetrical cadence
Several consecutive sentences have similar length, rhythm, or clause structure.
This often makes prose sound manufactured even when each sentence is technically fine.

### 6. Rephrased repetition
The text repeats the same idea with near-synonyms instead of advancing the argument.

### 7. Empty confidence
The prose sounds polished and certain without showing where the certainty comes from.

### 8. Missing local specificity
The text could fit almost any company, product, page, or audience because it lacks grounded detail from the surrounding context.

### 9. Overqualified tone
The passage overuses softeners or intensifiers such as:
- very
- really
- quite
- truly
- essentially
- basically
- notably
- significantly

### 10. Safe ending paragraphs
The ending summarizes in generic terms instead of landing on a concrete implication, decision, or action.

## Non-signals

Do not treat the following as evidence by themselves:
- correct grammar
- neutral tone
- short sentences
- formal style
- standard business vocabulary
- non-native phrasing
- template-driven copy in regulated or support contexts

## Decision Rule

Do not ask "Was this written by AI?"
Ask:
1. What makes this passage feel generic, padded, or context-thin?
2. Which parts would a careful human editor cut, sharpen, or ground?
3. What is the smallest rewrite that fixes that?
